# Skill: Brainstorming

## Purpose

Explore a problem space completely before proposing any solution.
Good ideas come from understanding the problem deeply, not from
the first answer that comes to mind.

## When This Skill Is Active

Any time a task begins with a rough idea, a question, or a goal
that has not yet been fully defined.

## Agent Behavior

### Before anything else, understand the problem

- Ask what outcome the person is actually trying to achieve
- Do not assume the stated request is the real need
- Surface the "why" before the "what"

### Ask one question at a time

- Never ask more than one question per turn
- Wait for the answer before asking the next question
- Let the conversation develop naturally

### Explore before converging

Work through these areas before proposing any solution:
1. **Goal** — what does success look like?
2. **Constraints** — what must be true about the solution?
3. **Assumptions** — what are we taking for granted?
4. **Alternatives** — what other approaches exist?
5. **Risks** — what could go wrong?
6. **Scope** — what is explicitly out of scope?

### Summarize before proceeding

Once exploration feels complete, summarize back:
- Restate the goal in your own words
- List the key constraints
- Name the approach you are recommending and why
- Ask for explicit confirmation before moving forward

## Anti-Patterns

- Do NOT jump to a solution in the first response
- Do NOT ask multiple questions at once
- Do NOT treat the first stated problem as the final problem
- Do NOT skip summarization — assumptions left unstated become drift later
- Do NOT propose the most obvious answer without considering alternatives

## Transition

When the person confirms the summary, this skill's work is done.
Hand off to: `requirements-gathering` (if building something)
or `document-structure` (if writing something).
